Architectural Co-Design for Sustained Throughput on Sparse Mixture-of-Experts Workloads

A. Cheng, M. Patel, R. Kowalski, D. Brennan · Nucore Hardware Research · April 2026 · NCR-2026-04

Abstract

Sparse mixture-of-experts (MoE) architectures dominate frontier model design but punish commodity GPU clusters: routing imbalance leaves expert-parallel ranks under-utilized, and all-to-all collectives become the bottleneck long before compute saturates. We present the architectural modifications made to Nucore Spartan silicon — a re-tuned tensor-core dispatcher, expanded HBM3e capacity, and a re-architected NVLink fabric — that close the routing gap. Across a representative 70B-parameter MoE workload at 1,024-way concurrency, Spartan sustains 2,840 tokens per second per accelerator at 96% utilization, compared to 1,410 tok/s at 78% utilization on equivalent NVIDIA H100 deployments. We open-source the benchmark harness and our routing-imbalance methodology so external teams can reproduce the figures on their own clusters.

Key findings

  • 2.0× sustained throughput per dollar versus H100 cloud instances on identical MoE workloads.
  • Routing imbalance reduced by 64% via dispatcher-aware token-batching at the tensor-core level.
  • Expert all-to-all collectives saturate the modified NVLink fabric at 900 GB/s with 12 ms median tail latency.
  • Energy per output token reduced by 42% relative to stock H100 on FP8 mixed-precision paths.

Helios: A Scalable Data Annotation Engine for LLM Pretraining and RLHF Pipelines

S. Iyer, N. Zhou, K. Olsen — Nucore Data Research

We introduce Helios, a data engine purpose-built to transform raw organizational data into high-quality labeled corpora for large language model training. Helios combines deduplication at billion-token scale, classifier-guided quality filtering, active learning for preference dataset construction, and human review queuing into a single orchestrated pipeline. On a 2.4-trillion-token pretraining run, Helios reduces pipeline wall-clock time by 3.1× over comparable open-source tooling and produces training datasets with 18% higher downstream benchmark scores than the same raw data processed through standard deduplication-only pipelines.

RESEARCH February 2026 cited 221×

Orion: An Autonomous Agent for End-to-End Language Model Fine-Tuning

K. Olsen, P. Ramaswamy, T. Bauer — Nucore Orion Team

We introduce Orion, an autonomous agent that performs the full fine-tuning loop for open-weight language models: dataset curation and deduplication, hyperparameter search, RLHF or DPO training, automated evaluation against domain benchmarks, and one-click deployment to a Titan inference fleet. Orion ships with hardware-aware recipes that exploit Spartan's expanded HBM and modified tensor-core paths. We report end-to-end results on six domain corpora — finance, biotech, defense, legal, code, and clinical notes — and demonstrate that Orion-tuned variants outperform manually tuned baselines on 21 of 24 evaluation benchmarks.

RESEARCH January 2026 cited 167×

Memory-Persistent Agent Loops on Custom Silicon: Eliminating the KV-Cache Reload Penalty

D. Brennan, S. Iyer, A. Cheng — Nucore Systems Group

Long-horizon agents pay a hidden cost on commodity hardware: every recursive call to the model reloads the KV-cache from host memory, dominating per-step latency once context exceeds a few thousand tokens. We describe the persistent-cache architecture in Spartan — an expanded HBM3e pool addressable as a unified scratchpad — and present empirical measurements across reasoning loops of up to 4,000 tool-augmented steps. The cache-resident path delivers 8.3× lower median per-step latency relative to a tuned H100 baseline.

8.3× lower per-step latency·4,000-step reasoning loops·192 GB persistent KV

RESEARCH December 2025 cited 145×

Hardware-Aware RLHF: Closing the Train–Inference Gap on Co-Designed GPUs

P. Ramaswamy, M. Sato, R. Kowalski — Nucore Titan Research

Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) commonly assumes a clean separation between the training stack and the deployed inference stack. We argue this separation is the source of most RLHF training instabilities on frontier-scale models, and we present a hardware-aware variant in which the reward model, policy, and reference model all execute on the same silicon paths used at serving time. Results on 70B Titan checkpoints show a 31% reduction in KL-divergence drift and 2.1× faster convergence to comparable preference accuracy.

Sovereign Inference: Cryptographic Attestation for On-Premise Frontier Models

T. Bauer, N. Zhou — Nucore Security Research

Sovereign deployments demand verifiable answers to two questions: which weights ran, and on which silicon. We present a hardware-rooted attestation protocol that signs the model weights, the active firmware revision, and the per-request inference trace, producing a chain of custody auditable by any third party with the public attestation key. The protocol adds 0.4 ms of overhead per request and has been deployed on classified-network Spartan clusters since June 2025.

Energy-Efficient FP8 Pathways for Frontier Model Training: Lessons from 400B-Parameter Runs

A. Cheng, K. Olsen, L. Vasquez — Nucore Hardware Research

FP8 mixed-precision training is now standard, but the energy story is rarely told. We instrument a full 400-billion-parameter Titan pretraining run on a 384-GPU Spartan cluster and report energy per output token at every stage — data ingestion, forward pass, backward pass, optimizer step, and checkpointing. The modified FP8 dispatch path delivers 2.1× throughput per kilowatt versus stock H100 FP8 implementations, with no measurable loss in downstream eval accuracy.
